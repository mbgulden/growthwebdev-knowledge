**DOCUMENT 2: PARTNER INTERVIEW & STORY EXTRACTION GUIDE**

### **Business Partner Interview: Extracting the Core of Your Brand**

A truly great website doesn't just list dates and prices—it builds immediate trust. Because personal safety, self-defense, and firearms training can feel intimidating, your website needs to feel incredibly welcoming, authoritative, and empowering the moment a woman lands on it.  
To capture your unique voice, please look through these questions together or answer them individually. Don't worry about perfect grammar or writing polished copy—just talk through your answers or jot down your thoughts in plain English. We will use your raw answers to train our AI system to write authentic website text that sounds exactly like you.

### **Section 1: The Origin Story & "The Why"**

1. **What sparked the idea to create a women-only defense and firearms company here in Meridian?** Was there a specific moment, conversation, or gap you noticed in the traditional co-ed gun range experience that made you realize this *needed* to exist?  
2. **For each partner:** What is your personal backstory with self-defense or firearms? What drives you to wake up and teach this to others?  
3. **Why "women-only"?** What happens to the energy, comfort level, and learning environment in a classroom when it is strictly a space for women, taught by people who understand their specific safety concerns?

### **Section 2: Understanding Your Students & Their Fears**

1. **Who is your typical student, and what is she feeling right before she signs up?** Is she a busy mom wanting to protect her kids, a young professional living on her own, or someone who recently moved to the Treasure Valley? Is she usually terrified, nervous, or just curious?  
2. **How do you handle fear or anxiety in your classes?** If a woman is completely intimidated by the noise or power of a handgun, what is your specific approach to helping her overcome that and feel in control?  
3. **What is the single biggest transformation you see in a student from the minute she walks into your classroom to the minute she finishes her live-fire range time?** (If you have a quick, anonymous story or example of a student who went from anxious to empowered, please share it\!)

### **Section 3: The Meridian & Idaho Context**

1. **Idaho has a strong firearm culture, but we also have a lot of newcomers moving to the area.** How do your classes cater to both lifelong Idahoans who want to step up their defense game, and people who are completely new to gun ownership and Idaho's specific laws?  
2. **When it comes to the Idaho Enhanced Concealed Carry license, why do you highly recommend it to women in our community?** What real-world legal and practical confidence does it give them when moving around Meridian, Boise, or traveling out of state?

### **Section 4: Website Style & Visual Vibe**

1. **When a woman visits your website, how should it feel visually?** \* *Option A:* Sleek, tactical, ultra-professional, and serious.  
   * *Option B:* Warm, community-focused, approachable, and deeply empowering.  
   * *Option C:* Clean, modern, minimalist, and athletic.  
2. **What are 3 websites (inside or outside your industry) whose look, colors, or overall "vibe" you love?** What do you like about them? Alternately, what is something you've seen on other local business websites that you absolutely want to avoid?
