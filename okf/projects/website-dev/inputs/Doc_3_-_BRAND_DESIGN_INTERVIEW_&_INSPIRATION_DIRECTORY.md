**DOCUMENT 3: BRAND DESIGN INTERVIEW & INSPIRATION DIRECTORY**

### **Visual Direction & Competitor Benchmarking Guide**

Now that we are gathering your core text and story, the next step is defining the exact look, feel, and visual personality of your new website.  
In the self-defense and firearms industry—especially when creating a dedicated, safe space for women—design choices carry immense weight. We want to completely avoid the aggressive, hyper-tactical, unapproachable look of traditional gun ranges, while still projecting absolute safety, precision, and elite professionalism.  
To help you pinpoint exactly what you want, this document combines a **Visual Preference Interview** with a **Real-World Inspiration Directory**. Please review the examples and questions below, and jot down your thoughts.

### **Part 1: Real-World Inspiration Directory**

Below are examples of how other businesses in Idaho, Utah, and across the country structure their firearms and safety training websites. We have highlighted standard regional leaders, as well as standout examples that cater specifically to women.  
As you click through these, pay attention to what you like (and what you don't like) regarding their colors, fonts, images, and how easy it is to find information.

#### **A. Direct "Women-Only" Benchmarks (The Gold Standards)**

These brands prove that a female-focused safety business succeeds by emphasizing empowerment, comfort, and community over militaristic or intimidating visuals.

* **Slade Defense (Utah):** [womentacticaldefense.com](https://www.womentacticaldefense.com/)  
  * *What to notice:* This organization is led by women and tailored exclusively to women. They use clean, premium branding, highly structured training paths (from 6-hour foundational courses up to advanced tactical tiers), and focus heavily on community safety.  
* **Concealed Coalition \- Ladies Only (Meridian, ID Hub):** [Meridian, ID Ladies Only Course](https://www.concealedcoalition.com/scheels/ladies-only-meridian/)  
  * *What to notice:* This is a national training network running highly localized, all-inclusive "experiences" out of the Meridian Scheels. Notice how their text positions the class as "comfortable, supportive, and non-judgmental."  
* **TNT Guns & Range \- Ladies Only Courses (Utah):** [tntgunrange.com/classes/ladies-only](https://tntgunrange.com/classes/ladies-only)  
  * *What to notice:* A great example of clear presentation. They explicitly list class limits (e.g., "9 People Max"), exact duration (3 hours), and gear requirements, while also showing how they layer lethal training with non-lethal options like pepper spray.  
* **Fernan Rod & Gun Club \- Mama Bears Course (North Idaho):** [frgc.org/women\_at\_frgc.php](https://www.frgc.org/women_at_frgc.php)  
  * *What to notice:* An excellent example of mission-driven, family-focused marketing. They speak directly to "Mama Bears" who want to protect their homes, shifting the narrative entirely toward maternal protection and community support.

#### **B. High-Volume Regional Leaders (Local Market Comparison)**

These sites represent the broader commercial landscape you will be competing with for local Google search traffic in the Idaho and Utah regions.

* **Armed Self Defense Institute (Utah):** [trainasdi.com](https://trainasdi.com/courses/utah-concealed-carry-permit)  
  * *What to notice:* This is a high-volume leader for the Utah permit. Their site is built for fast, frictionless sign-ups: clear, upfront pricing ($45) and a very simple, step-by-step layout of what the state requires.  
* **U.S. Concealed Carry Association (USCCA) \- Idaho Directory:** [usconcealedcarry.com/firearms-training/idaho](https://www.usconcealedcarry.com/firearms-training/classes/womens-firearm-training/idaho/)  
  * *What to notice:* This is a massive national aggregator site. Looking at their local Meridian listings helps show the industry standard for curriculum definitions and what local audiences see when searching for basic training.
